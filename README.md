

**Data files for structural systems biology - Levan Biosynthesis**

Reference: **Structural and Boolean Network Modeling of the Levan Biosynthetic Pathway in _Bacillus subtilis_**

Aruldoss Immanuel 1 , Dharshini Priya S 2 , Danthuluri Sree Lakshmi 2 , Venkatasubramanian Ulaganathan 1,2*, Ragothaman M. Yennamalli 2*
(*Corresponding Authors)

**Abstract**
Motivation: _Bacillus subtilis_ is a key organism in industrial biotechnology for producing value-added products. This study enhances the metabolic model of _B. subtilis_ by incorporating structural information, focusing on levan biosynthesisâ€”a fructose polymer with applications in hydrogels, drug delivery, and wound healing. We employed structural systems biology to integrate known structural details into the _B. subtilis_ metabolic pathway, creating a structure-annotated genome-scale metabolic model (GEM). 
Results: This manually curated model links proteins and reactions to PDB entries, providing structural perspectives previously overlooked in GEMs. We quantified structural coverage: 331/1250 reactions (26.5%) and 508 PDB structures mapped to 168 UniProt IDs, with focus coverage of _sacB, sacC, sacX/Y, levD/E/F/G_, and _sacP_. The structural layer does not alter stoichiometry or constraints unless explicitly parameterized. This structure-annotated resource enables systematic testing of phenotype predictions and design strategies. The structure-based metabolic model advances understanding of levan production and microbial metabolism, facilitating sustainable and efficient biotechnological processes for industrial applications.
