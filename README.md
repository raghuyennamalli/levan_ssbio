

**Data files for structural systems biology - Levan Biosynthesis**

This repository contains Protein Data Bank structure (.pdb) files of Bacillus subtilis assciated with iyo844 model 
ABStract : Abstract
Motivation: Bacillus subtilis is a key organism in industrial biotechnology for producing value-added products. This study enhances the metabolic model of B. subtilis by incorporating structural information, focusing on levan biosynthesisâ€”a fructose polymer with applications in hydrogels, drug delivery, and wound healing. We employed structural systems biology to integrate known structural details into the B. subtilis metabolic pathway, creating a structure-annotated genome-scale metabolic model (GEM). 
Results: This manually curated model links proteins and reactions to PDB entries, providing structural perspectives previously overlooked in GEMs. We quantified structural coverage: 331/1250 reactions (26.5%) and 508 PDB structures mapped to 168 UniProt IDs, with focus coverage of sacB, sacC, sacX/Y, levD/E/F/G, and sacP. The structural layer does not alter stoichiometry or constraints unless explicitly parameterized. This structure-annotated resource enables systematic testing of phenotype predictions and design strategies. The structure-based metabolic model advances understanding of levan production and microbial metabolism, facilitating sustainable and efficient biotechnological processes for industrial applications.
